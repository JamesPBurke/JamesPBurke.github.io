I want to tell you something about what teaching is like. _For me,_ I guess. I see that world from within my classroom, which is a little, strange domain. Denizens show up. They are all kinda of young people. All kinds. 

>_"This class is weird."_ 

A freshman said that to me in the fall of 2020. I don't even really remember which of my classes it was. But I told him, "I know." I was in the middle of setting up a Raspberry PI for him to do an assignment. This was the year of multiple technology failures. During the first summer of COVID, the district systems were hit with a Russian ransomware attack rendering my computer lab inexplicably inoperable for 6+ months. At the same time, a lack of effective forecasting meant our students didn't have Chromebooks. And while they were allowed to bring in their own devices from home at that time, some didn't have anything like that.

[![A Raspberry PI](/assets/images/uploads/pexels-alessandro-oliverio-1472443.jpg "Photo by Alessandro Oliverio on Pexels")](https://www.pexels.com/photo/silver-and-green-circuit-board-1472443/)

I won't go into detail about the Raspberry PI device, but I was trying to do what I could to prevent a reluctant student from entirely checking out. I fiddled with a board and wires and Wi-Fi and doing possibly slightly unauthorized things (I tell you this in confidence)... I gave him a tiny screen to work on and at least he was no longer unable to access the work. 

And it must have been a bit of a sight, because that's when we had the exchange I mentioned. 

After a pause, I said, "Why is it weird?"
